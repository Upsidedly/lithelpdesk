Essay Questions

These were not reviewed. Quality may vary.

Family and Home Life

  1. Families can either nurture or hinder a child’s development.
    Write an essay in which you describe the family life of ONE major character. In your essay, explain how this family dynamic influences the character’s development and discuss ONE theme the writer uses to highlight the role of family.
  2. Children often carry the burden of responsibility within dysfunctional homes.
    Write an essay in which you describe Anjanee’s home life. In your essay, assess how her responsibilities affect her mental health and evaluate ONE technique the author uses to portray this strain.
  3. Parental figures shape the direction of a child’s life.
    Write an essay in which you compare the parenting styles of TWO adults from the novel. In your essay, assess how their approach affects ONE child and discuss how the author uses contrast to make this point.
  4. Migration can redefine relationships within a family.
    Write an essay in which you explore the effects of Mama Patsy's migration. In your essay, analyze how this separation affects both Lacey and Mama Patsy, and discuss ONE way the author uses letters to explore this theme.

Friendship and Personal Relationships

  1. Friendships can provide support or become strained under pressure.
    Write an essay in which you describe the friendship between Lacey and Anjanee. In your essay, examine how this relationship evolves over time and discuss ONE event or symbol that reflects its transformation.
  2. A friend’s death can become a defining moment in a young person’s life.
    Write an essay in which you describe how Anjanee’s death affects Lacey. In your essay, explore how Lacey responds to this event and analyze ONE narrative technique that highlights the emotional impact.
  3. Minor characters often influence the growth of the protagonist.
    Write an essay in which you select TWO minor characters. In your essay, explain how their actions affect Lacey and discuss ONE technique the author uses to emphasize their influence.

Gender and Societal Expectations

  1. Teachers can empower or discourage students through their actions.
    Write an essay in which you compare Mr. Joseph and Mrs. Lopez. In your essay, explain how each teacher shapes the students’ self-perception and examine ONE literary device the author uses to portray their impact.
  2. Gender inequality is a pervasive force in the characters’ lives.
    Write an essay in which you describe the expectations placed on women in the novel. In your essay, analyze how TWO characters respond to these expectations and discuss ONE way the author challenges these norms.
  3. Education is both a path to freedom and a site of conflict.
    Write an essay in which you describe how TWO characters seek education. In your essay, explain the obstacles they face and discuss ONE literary element that shows how education empowers or limits them.

Dreams, Identity, and Personal Growth

  1. Dreams can guide characters toward action and resilience.
    Write an essay in which you describe the dream or goal of ONE character. In your essay, analyze how they pursue this dream and examine ONE challenge that threatens their success.
  2. Internal conflict can lead to significant character growth.
    Write an essay in which you explore ONE major internal struggle faced by Lacey. In your essay, explain how she responds to this conflict and evaluate ONE literary technique used to show her growth.
  3. Major characters help to shape the direction of a story.
    Write an essay in which you describe the role of ONE major character. In your essay, examine how their actions influence the plot and discuss ONE theme that is revealed through their decisions.
  4. Dreams can reflect a character’s emotional or psychological state.
    Write an essay in which you describe Lacey’s dream about her Geography book. In your essay, explain what this dream suggests about her feelings and examine ONE literary device used to convey this symbolism.

Social Issues: Prejudice, Race, and Class

  1. People from rural backgrounds often face social bias.
    Write an essay in which you describe the prejudice Lacey faces at school. In your essay, explain how her rural identity affects her experience and discuss ONE way the author critiques this bias.
  2. Prejudice influences the choices and behaviors of characters.
    Write an essay in which you describe ONE form of prejudice present in the novel. In your essay, analyze how it affects a major character and examine ONE symbol or situation that reflects this prejudice.
  3. Race and class divisions affect students both inside and outside the classroom.
    Write an essay in which you explore how Lacey and Anjanee are treated at school. In your essay, discuss how racial or class bias impacts their experiences and explain ONE literary device used to highlight inequality.
  4. Symbols can reveal underlying social values or tensions.
    Write an essay in which you analyze TWO symbols related to family or class. In your essay, explain how they reflect broader societal expectations and discuss ONE technique used to emphasize their meaning.
