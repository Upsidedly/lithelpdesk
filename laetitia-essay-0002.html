Life of Laetitia Essay #0002

Parental figures shape the direction of a child’s life.
Write an essay in which you compare the parenting styles of TWO adults from the novel. In your essay, assess how their approach affects ONE child and discuss how the author uses contrast to make this point.

In For the Life of Laetitia by Merle Hodge, the contrasting parenting styles of Cephas and Ma significantly shape the development of Michael, Cephas’ son. Cephas is authoritarian and emotionally neglectful, while Ma is nurturing and firm. The impact of their parenting is most evident in the difference between Michael and Lacey, Cephas’ children, and in Michael’s growth after he is influenced by Ma’s values through Lacey. The author uses contrast to underscore the transformative power of positive parenting and the consequences of neglect.

Cephas is a controlling and emotionally absent father. Initially uninterested in Lacey’s life, he only shows interest when she gains entrance to a government secondary school. He exercises harsh authority in his household and expects obedience without nurturing emotional support. Cephas’ approach stifles Lacey’s growth and well-being, and his household environment contributes to her eventual nervous breakdown. Meanwhile, his son Michael—raised entirely under Cephas' influence—is rude, untidy, inconsiderate, and unhelpful. He lacks the discipline and emotional intelligence that would help him relate respectfully to others.

By contrast, Ma, Lacey’s grandmother, is the respected matriarch in her rural household. Her parenting style is characterized by love, discipline, and encouragement. She ensures that Lacey is well-behaved, neat, and considerate, instilling in her the importance of education and self-worth. When Lacey comes to live in Cephas’ household during term time, she carries with her Ma’s values and begins to influence Michael positively. Lacey gently but firmly teaches him to be tidier and more considerate. Through this, we see Michael begin to change, proving that environment and guidance, not just blood, shape a child’s behavior.

The author uses this contrast in parenting styles to highlight the novel’s theme: children thrive under support and discipline, but suffer under neglect. Cephas’ harshness produces a son with poor manners and a daughter who experiences a breakdown, while Ma’s love produces a strong, hopeful child who tries to help others, including Michael. The transformation in Michael, sparked by Lacey’s presence, reinforces this point: given the right guidance, even a neglected child can begin to change.

In conclusion, Merle Hodge demonstrates that the direction of a child’s life is deeply influenced by the nature of their parental figures. The contrast between Cephas and Ma highlights this: Cephas’ emotional absence fosters dysfunction, while Ma’s warmth fosters strength. Through this, the author shows that love, support, and values—not authority alone—are what truly shape a child.
