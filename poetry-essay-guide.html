Poetry Essay Guide

Question Structure

All poetry questions have three parts:

  1. Describe what happens in each poem.
  2. Discuss the speaker’s attitude.
  3. Analyze one device used to convey meaning.

Essay Planning

  • Identify the theme.
  • Select two poems (if not provided).
  • Note: brief summary, tone/attitude, and one device for each poem.

Introduction Structure

  1. Hook: A general statement about the theme.
  2. Poems: State both poems and link to the theme.
  3. Summary sentence for part 1.
  4. Summary sentence for part 2.
  5. Summary sentence for part 3.

Example Introduction

Women are frequently subject to unequal treatment. The poems “A Stone’s Throw” and “The Woman Speaks to the Man Who Has Employed Her Son” both explore gendered power and vulnerability. The former portrays a woman attacked by a violent mob. The latter shows a mother’s inability to protect her son. Both speakers reveal concern. Imagery and tone emphasize injustice and powerlessness.

Body Paragraph Structure (for each part)

Each paragraph should compare both poems for one part of the question. Use the P.E.E. structure:

  • Point: State your idea clearly.
  • Example: Quote from the poem.
  • Explanation: Explain how it supports the point.

Example Part 1 Paragraph

“A Stone’s Throw”: The woman is physically assaulted — “we dragged her, by her hair.”

“The Woman Speaks…”: A mother pleads for her son’s freedom — “She is praying.”

Devices to Analyze (Part 3)

  • Imagery: Descriptive language that appeals to senses.
  • Personification: Human qualities to non-human elements.
  • Repetition: Emphasizes an idea or emotion.
  • Symbolism: Represents larger ideas.
  • Contrast: Juxtaposes two opposing ideas.

Example Device Analysis

“A Stone’s Throw”: Imagery — the “crowd” creates a violent visual of mob justice.

“The Woman Speaks…”: Tone — desperate and pleading to emphasize powerlessness.

Conclusion Structure

  1. Restate the theme.
  2. Summarize part 1 (event).
  3. Summarize part 2 (attitude).
  4. Summarize part 3 (device).

Example Conclusion

Both poems portray the oppression of women. In “A Stone’s Throw”, the woman is violently punished. In “The Woman Speaks…”, the mother is powerless. Both speakers reject this treatment. Imagery and tone highlight injustice and social failure.

Linking Phrases

  • Similarly
  • In contrast
  • On the other hand
  • Both poems show
  • Whereas

Essay Checklist

  • 3 full body paragraphs (1 for each part of the question)
  • Both poems discussed in every paragraph
  • Clear structure: intro, body, conclusion
  • At least one quote per poem per paragraph
  • No plot summary unless directly relevant
